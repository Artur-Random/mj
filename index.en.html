From the Author

Seventeen years ago, the King of Pop, Michael Jackson, passed away.

A man who forever changed the very meaning of the words “music,” “dance,” and “fashion.” A man who set a bar that no one has managed to clear to this day — and, I am certain, no one ever will. A man whose name and whose voice have been heard by almost everyone on this planet.

The high-energy “Beat It” and the sharply social “They Don’t Care About Us”, the piercing “You Are Not Alone” and the legendary “Billie Jean”… Michael’s contribution to world culture cannot be measured in dry metrics. It can only be measured in genuine, heartfelt love and emotion — be it joy, goosebumps, or tears.

On June 25, 2009, the world lost a musical genius, a loving father, a noble philanthropist, and simply an incredibly bright and kind Human Being.

Thank you for everything, Michael.

Rest in peace, MJ 👑

Smile, though your heart is aching

About the Project

The idea of getting to the truth — reconstructing the last days of Michael’s life, learning everything down to the smallest detail — first came to me back in January 2023, and for almost all of 2023 I studied his biography, watched documentaries, read about the accusations and their rebuttals, and researched in detail Michael’s trips to Russia in 1993 and 1996. But then other matters and other tasks piled up, and I shelved all the materials for a long while…

But in 2026, the film Michael — the one I had been waiting for so long — finally came out. On June 3, 2026, I went to see it in very pleasant company, and it hit me with such a powerful emotional “boom” that I decided to return to the materials and see this through to the end. And so, by the 17th anniversary of Michael’s death, I made it. I did it.

It feels symbolic that it is the 17th anniversary: where I come from, in the post-Soviet world, there is a settled notion that a person is still a teenager up to and including 17, and at 18 adult life abruptly begins. Even at 50, Michael remained that very teenager inside… a child whose childhood had been taken away.

This whole project is only a small part of what I would like to do in Michael’s memory. And I am not stopping here — there is a lot of work ahead!

You can be a winner but you gotta keep the faith!

Artur Random

At the scene, Murray stated the patient had been unconscious for "about a minute" before help was called. Transport to the Medical Center UCLA
Jackson was taken to Ronald Reagan UCLA Medical Center. Resuscitation was continued by Dr. Richelle Cooper, Dr. Thao Nguyen, and the emergency team. According to the physicians' testimony, Murray once again did not disclose the propofol. At 12:57, having been reached by radio at the hospital by paramedic Senneff on the scene, Dr. Cooper was prepared to authorize pronouncing death, but Murray insisted on continuing resuscitation and transporting the patient to the hospital, assuming responsibility for further treatment.

~13:14–14:26
UCLA: Murray unable to state the timeline Nguyen's testimony
UCLA cardiologist Thao Nguyen directly asked Murray for the time the drug was administered, the time he found the patient not breathing, and the interval before the 911 call. Murray could not answer any of the three questions, saying he had "no concept of time" and did not have a watch. In Nguyen's assessment, the situation did not look like a case of too little being done, but a case of help arriving too late.

The Window of Danger: Synergy and Absence of Monitoring

Benzodiazepine accumulation zone
01:30 → 07:30
Three drugs successively depress the CNS before the previous one is fully cleared; the cumulative effect builds.
↓
Critical moment: propofol added
~10:40
Propofol on top of accumulated benzodiazepines — synergistic depression of the respiratory center. The self-administration theory was "based on speculation" — appellate court, B237677.
↓
Monitoring gap window
10:34 → ~11:56
Murray was making phone calls (AT&T/Sprint billing); the pulse oximeter had no audible alarm; there was no capnograph. Per the manufacturer's representative (Nonin Medical), the 9500 model has no audible alarm and is designed only for brief ~10-second spot checks, not continuous monitoring; it provides no benefit unless someone is continuously watching the display from within the same room.
↓
Crisis discovered
~11:56–12:20
One-handed CPR on a soft surface; before the 911 call — an order to put the vials away.

Findings of the coroner's consultant anesthesiologist · Dr. Selma Calmes · 3 Aug 2009

  • The IV catheter was placed in the left leg. The injection port of the line was located 13.5 cm from the catheter tip in the vein.
  • To administer the drug himself, Jackson would have had to sharply bend his knee or sit up — an extremely awkward position while falling asleep.
  • The standard of care for propofol administration was not met: there was no pump for precise dosing, no continuous observation, no monitor with an alarm.
  • The propofol levels in the toxicology are consistent with general anesthesia for major abdominal surgery, with infusion delivery following a bolus.
  • Lorazepam was present at a pharmacologically significant concentration and intensified the respiratory and cardiac depression caused by the propofol.

Dr. Steven Shafer · prosecution expert · People v. Murray · appeal B237677  ·  Dr. Selma Calmes · Anesthesiology Consultant · LA County Coroner · 3 Aug 2009

Would It Have Changed the Outcome?

Asked directly by the defense whether knowing that Jackson had been given 25 mg of propofol around 10:30 would have changed her treatment, UCLA physician Richelle Cooper answered no, adding that the patient had died long before he became her patient. This statement concerns the outcome of treatment at UCLA and does not contradict the appellate finding in B237677 that withholding information about the propofol "significantly hampered" the medical response at an earlier stage — at the scene and during transport. These are two claims of different scope: one about the final outcome at the hospital, the other about the overall course of care.

As reported by ABC News, Oct 3, 2011, citing the testimony of Richelle Cooper, M.D.

Open Questions

What Remains Unanswered

01
Where did 243 of the 255 vials of propofol go?
Only 12 were found at the scene. The fate of 243 has not been established. DEA FOIA filed — June 2026.
02
Whom did Murray call between 10:14 and 11:07?
Four calls appear in the billing records — the identities of the parties have not been established in the public case materials.
03
Was the propofol administered by infusion rather than a 25 mg bolus?
Dr. Shafer (prosecution expert): the concentrations indicate a prolonged infusion. The theory of self-administration by Jackson is "based on speculation" — appellate court.
04
Did Lieutenant Streng order the investigation limited to Murray alone?
Detective Smith testified under oath that such an order existed. No documentary confirmation is publicly available. No FOIA filed with the LAPD.
05
The results of the DEA investigation into Jackson's other doctors?
The DEA investigated Klein and others. The results were never published. FOIA filed with the DEA — response pending.

Supplementary investigation · outside case 2009-04415

Accusations
and Acquittal

A separate biographical and reputational context: the 1993 allegations, the 2005 criminal trial, the jury's not-guilty verdict, and the media accusations that followed Michael Jackson's death.

Artur Random

Section principle

This section is not part of the investigation into Michael Jackson's 2009 death. It records a separate biographical and reputational context: the 1993 allegations, the 2005 criminal trial, the jury's not-guilty verdict, and the subsequent media accusations that emerged after Michael Jackson's death.

Important: the claims in this section are not presented as established facts. In 2005, Michael Jackson was acquitted on all counts of the criminal indictment. The purpose of this section is to separate court outcomes, documents and verifiable facts from rumors, media interpretations and sensationalized coverage.

Legal outcome
Acquitted on all counts in 2005

This block is presented as a supplementary investigation and reputational context, not as part of the reconstruction of the 2009 death.

Case type
Civil (criminal case closed)
No criminal charges were filed. Settlement of ~$23 million, January 1994. Initiated by Jackson's team.
Accuser
Jordan Chandler, age 13
Never testified in criminal court. Further details of his private life are not included in the project's verified corpus.
Key document
Declaration of J. Chandler, 28 Dec 1993
A legal declaration made under penalty of perjury. Describes a gradual escalation in detail.
Established
The father's financial motive is documented
Audio recording of Evan Chandler: "I will get everything I want." He had earlier demanded Jackson's help with a film script. Attorney Barry Rothman — debts to 30+ creditors.
Established
The settlement was initiated by Jackson's team
Confirmed by attorney Larry Feldman's sworn testimony · trial, April 1, 2005.
Contradiction
The description of the body as "circumcised" contradicts the 2009 autopsy
Official autopsy report (Dr. Christopher Rogers, June 26, 2009, p. 5): "The penis appears uncircumcised". This directly contradicts Jordan Chandler's declaration of December 28, 1993, in which the description of the anatomical features was different. Source: primary coroner's document, case 2009-04415, p. 5 — an established medical fact. Attorney Feldman blocked the use of the body-search photographs (December 20, 1993) in the civil court.
Not established
Did the testimony match the body-search photographs (December 20, 1993)?
The results are sealed. The photographs were blocked from court. No access.
Case
People v. Jackson No. 1133603
Acquitted on all 14 counts. June 13, 2005.
Systemic link 1993–2005
One attorney · one psychologist · one prosecutor
Feldman (contingency fee), Katz (double conflict of interest), Sneddon (both cases). Feldman admitted: a guilty verdict would automatically have won the civil suit.
Accuser
Gavin Arvizo, age 13
A documented history of manipulation by the family. Testimony changed between DCFS, the grand jury and the trial.
Established
The 2005 acquittal was legally well-founded
The Arvizo family: a history of manipulation (JCPenney $152K, welfare fraud — Fifth Amendment). Most prosecution witnesses had financial claims against Jackson. Psychologist Katz did not check the family's history before evaluating the testimony.
Established beyond dispute
Alcohol given to minors
Three independent sources: Salas, Arvizo, Montgomery. Wine disguised in soda cans.
Established beyond dispute
Restriction of the Arvizo family's freedom
Three independent confirmations. Gate log entry: "the children are not allowed to leave." One-way tickets to Brazil (Montgomery's testimony).
Film
Leaving Neverland (2019, HBO)
Accusations by Wade Robson and James Safechuck. Directed by Dan Reed.
Civil suit
$400M · MJJ Productions and MJJ Ventures
Not against Jackson personally. Postponed to February 14, 2028.
Robson's suit
Dismissed (2013–2015)
The claims were rewritten 4 times. Judge Beckloff's ruling of May 26, 2015: Robson had known about the estate since February 2011 and lied about it.
Established
Robson twice denied abuse under oath in 2005
Trial transcript, May 5, 2005. Changed his position in 2013. Three different versions of "why he stayed silent": shame → did not realize → feared the reaction. Diary entries: "the time has come to get what is mine."
Architectural refutation
Neverland's main train station did not exist in 1988–1992
Construction blueprints: stamp "APPROVED SEP 11 1993", Santa Barbara County. Completed in 1994. Jackson first saw it in 1995. Safechuck claimed the abuse took place there in 1988–1992.
Chronological inconsistency
The 1989 Grammys were held in Los Angeles, not New York
Safechuck: abuse in a New York hotel during the 1989 Grammys. The 1989 Grammys — LA Shrine Auditorium. Jackson did not perform.
Physical-evidence inconsistency
The rings "from Jackson" are the same model as at the 2019 Emmys
The ring scene was shot last — 17 months after the rest of the material. Different furniture in the frame. The rings fit an adult finger.
Appeal B237677 · People v. Murray · 15 Jan 2014

What the court said: the wording of the ruling

The court affirmed the conviction unanimously on January 15, 2014. 68 pages. Below are the court's official findings, not interpretations.

Standard of criminal negligence
Murray's actions constituted gross negligence, not medical error; the criterion is a deviation from the standard of reasonable care to a degree creating a high risk of death.
B237677 · pp. 18–24
Self-administration of propofol — rejected
The defense theory was "based on speculation" and unsupported by evidence; the argument was rejected as untenable.
B237677 · pp. 31–35
Misinforming the UCLA medical team
Murray told neither the paramedics nor UCLA about the propofol; this significantly hampered resuscitation.
B237677 · pp. 26–28
Absence of proper equipment
Propofol was administered without a capnograph, without an alarmed monitor, without resuscitation equipment, without an assistant.
B237677 · pp. 20–22
Causation
A direct link between Murray's actions and the death was established; Jackson's drug dependence did not relieve Murray of responsibility.
B237677 · pp. 36–42
